Analytic Number Theory

Introduction to Analytic Number Theory

Analytic number theory uses methods from mathematical analysis to study number-theoretic problems involving integers and prime numbers.

It explores deep connections between complex analysis, number theory, and algebraic geometry.

Prime Number Theorem

Statement of the Prime Number Theorem (PNT)

The Prime Number Theorem states that the number of primes less than or equal to a given number x, denoted by π(x), is asymptotically equivalent to x / log x.

As x approaches infinity, π(x) / (x / log x) approaches 1.

Riemann Zeta Function

Definition and Basic Properties

The Riemann zeta function ζ(s) is defined for Re(s) > 1 by the series ζ(s) = ∑_{n=1}^∞ 1/n^s.

It has applications in number theory, particularly in understanding the distribution of prime numbers.

Dirichlet's Theorem on Primes in Arithmetic Progressions

Dirichlet's theorem states that for any two coprime integers a and b, there are infinitely many primes of the form a + bn, where n is a non-negative integer.

This theorem is fundamental in understanding the distribution of primes in arithmetic progressions.

Modular Forms and L-functions

Definition and Properties

Modular forms are complex analytic functions that satisfy certain transformation properties under modular group actions.

L-functions are complex functions associated with Dirichlet series that generalize the Riemann zeta function and have deep connections to number theory.

Elliptic Curves

Elliptic curves are algebraic curves defined by equations of the form y^2 = x^3 + ax + b, where a and b are constants.

They play a crucial role in modern number theory, particularly in the study of rational points and the Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer conjecture.

Diophantine Equations

Diophantine equations are polynomial equations with integer solutions. They include linear equations and more complex forms like Pell's equation.

These equations have been studied extensively in number theory, often using analytic techniques to find solutions and explore their properties.

Current Research and Open Problems

Analytic number theory continues to be an active area of research with many unsolved problems, such as the Riemann Hypothesis and questions about the distribution of primes.

Research in this field explores new connections between analysis, geometry, and algebra to deepen our understanding of number theoretic phenomena.
