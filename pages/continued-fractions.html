Continued Fractions

Introduction to Continued Fractions

Continued fractions are expressions of the form:

a0 + 1/(a1 + 1/(a2 + 1/(a3 + ... )))

where a0 is an integer and all other ai are positive integers. They provide a way to represent numbers through a sequence of integer quotients.

Theorems and Properties of Continued Fractions

Convergents of Continued Fractions

The convergents of a continued fraction are the fractions obtained by truncating the continued fraction at various points. They provide increasingly accurate rational approximations of the continued fraction.

  • The n-th convergent of the continued fraction is denoted by [a0; a1, a2, ..., an].
  • The convergents of a continued fraction for a number are the best rational approximations to the number.

Periodic Continued Fractions

A continued fraction is periodic if the sequence of quotients repeats after some point. Such fractions represent quadratic irrationals. If the continued fraction of a number is eventually periodic, then the number is a quadratic irrational.

Representation of Irrational Numbers

Every irrational number can be uniquely represented by an infinite continued fraction. For example, the golden ratio φ is represented by the simple continued fraction [1; 1, 1, 1, ...].

Examples of Continued Fractions

Square Roots

The continued fraction representation of the square root of a non-square positive integer is periodic. For example:

√2 = [1; 2, 2, 2, ...]

Euler's Continued Fraction for e

The number e has the continued fraction representation:

e = [2; 1, 2, 1, 1, 4, 1, 1, 6, ...]

Calculation of Continued Fractions

You can calculate the continued fraction representation of a number by repeatedly taking the integer part and the reciprocal of the fractional part.
